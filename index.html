June Lecture Meeting

Monday, 18th June, 2018 at 6:30 p.m. 
Shibuya Kyōiku Gakuen, Shibuya 1-21-18 , Shibuya-ku, Tokyo 150-0002

Mr. Alexander Byrne 

 “The S. Shobey Silk Store of Yokohama, its production and export of Western style gowns with fine Japanese embroidery as well as silk accessories from the Bakumatsu era onwards.”

The S. Shobey Silk Store was the first Japanese brand to design, manufacture, and sell a range of silk products including luxurious gowns, smoking jackets, handkerchiefs, neckties, and other embroidered items aimed at the Western market both in Japan and overseas. The company was established by Mr. Shobei Shiino (1839-1900) in 1859, the year Yokohama opened as a treaty port, when the young Mr. Shiino placed an English sign outside his store on Honcho-dori, Yokohama, that read ‘S. Shobey Silk Store’. In 1873 he was sent by the Meiji government to the World’s Fair in Vienna to learn about the silk industry in Europe. He then exhibited at subsequent World's Fairs in Philadelphia (1876), Paris (1878), Melbourne (1880), Barcelona (1888) and Chicago (1893), winning numerous awards for his gowns and other embroidered items.

The recent discovery of a silk embroidery made to commemorate Queen Victoria becoming Empress of India in 1877 further attests to the demand for S. Shobey products and their high quality. There is also evidence that S. Shobey was the first in the world to make decorative scarves. This visual talk takes a look at the S. Shobey Silk Store, its history and development, the story behind how the speaker came to know about it, as well as some interesting discoveries made along the way. Some early S. Shobey products will also be on display.

Alexander Byrne was born in Sydney in 1972 and first came to Japan on a working holiday in 1993. He graduated from Macquarie University (Sydney) in 1998 with a Bachelor of Arts (Japanese Studies) and spent one year as an exchange student at Tokyo Metropolitan University in 1999-2000 for his honours year while also working part-time at Berlitz Japan. In 2016 he left Berlitz after having reached the title of Senior Manager of Instruction and founded Bakumatsuya Co. Ltd. (www.bakumatsuya.com), a company dealing in rare books, manuscripts, and photographs mostly from the Edo and Meiji eras. His main interest is in items related to the communication between Westerners and Japanese people during that period. He also addressed the Asiatic Society of Japan in February 2011 on the topic of Japanese crepe paper books.
